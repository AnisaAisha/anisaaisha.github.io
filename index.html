About

I currently serve as a Dean's Fellow at Habib University, in Karachi, Pakistan. I hold a Masters in Computer Science with Computer Graphics specialization from Georgia Institute of Technology. I am extremely passionate about computer graphics, animation, data visualization and creative web development, which was the focus of my degree. I also have prior experience in the industry as a software developer, and am equipped with technical, communication and leadership skills. Additionally, I have been awarded the Fulbright Scholarship for the my graduate school program, and have served as the President of Fulbright Student Association at Georgia Tech.

Research Interests

I have been a part of JANUS Lab at Georgia Tech, working on a VR-based project for the rehabilitation of people with cognitive impairment issues. I have also been supervised by Prof. Greg Turk on research related to real-time mesh repair during 3D scanning. In general, my research interests include graphics rendering, mesh processing, and video game programming, with a particular emphasis on using 3D models and AR/VR technology to develop innovative solutions for real-world problems. Additionally, in the past I have worked on mesh processing and graphics pipeline research during my undergraduate degree.
