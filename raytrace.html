Ray Tracing

CS 6491: Fundamentals of Computer Graphics

During Spring 2024, I took a course on fundamentals of computer graphics at Georgia Tech. In this course, we primarily worked on a Whitted style ray tracer in Processing (Java), and creating 3D models using various implicit surface techniques. The major components of the ray tracer were,

  • Scene graphs
  • Anti-aliasing
  • Support for shadow rays
  • Diffuse and specular shading including reflections
  • Transformations (including rotation, scaling, and translation)
  • Soft effects such as motion blur, depth of field and glossy reflections
  • Acceleration structure for faster rendering of complex meshes (Bounding Volume Hierarchies)

A classic ray tracer is can only produce simple shadows and materials with diffuse shading. This project also implements the distributed ray tracing algorithm, which shoots multiple rays per pixel to implement several effects that cannot be achieved using a simple ray tracer such as anti-aliasing, soft shadows, motion blur, glossy reflections and depth of field.
